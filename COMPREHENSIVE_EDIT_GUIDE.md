**Location:** NEW SCENE after sentencing, before final chapter

```
## Epilogue: Rachel — Six Weeks After Sentencing

The psychiatric facility was cleaner than she'd expected. Brighter. Not the gray institutional horror she'd imagined. The reception area could have been a hospital, a university. Normal, until you noticed the locks.

They brought Josh to a meeting room with a reinforced window overlooking a courtyard. He looked different. Smaller. The confidence she'd known—the charm that had sold three books and a successful podcast—was gone.

"Rachel." His voice was hoarse. "You came."

She sat across the table from him. Did not offer her hand.

Josh was quiet for a long moment.

"All of it was me," he said finally. "That's the worst part. Marcus isn't a separate person. He's just me, without the walls. Me, without the parts that know how to stop." He looked at his hands. "Every word I wrote was mine. Every success we had together. It was all real. And so was everything else."

Rachel felt something crack behind her sternum.

"Lily Ashford was twenty-two years old," she said. "She wanted to work in forest conservation. She had a journal full of hopes and a sister who loved her. And you took all of that."

"I know."

"Do you? Do you actually understand?"

Josh met her eyes. No excuse in them. No plea for sympathy.

"I understand," he said. "I will spend the rest of my life understanding. That's all I can do now. Remember."

Rachel stood. There was nothing left to say.

At the door, she turned back.

"Eleanor's package arrived," she said. "I read everything. Every file. Every photo. Every recording."

Josh waited.

"She loved you," Rachel said. "Even knowing what you were. That was the cruelest thing of all."

She left without looking back.
```
