## Changes Implemented

### 1. CRITICAL: "Body Remembers" Dissociation Language (Line ~828)
**Issue:** Classic dissociation foreshadowing made Josh=Marcus too obvious by Chapter 2-3.

**Before:**
```
Something cold moved through my chest when I read that entry. A strange tightness, like my body remembered something my mind didn't. I stared at the words—*east wing, case never solved*—and for a moment the library felt too quiet, too still.

Then the feeling passed. Just another tragedy in a house full of them. I kept reading.
```

**After:**
```
I paused at that entry. An unsolved murder—exactly the kind of material I'd come here for. The podcast practically wrote itself: atmospheric location, tragic history, questions that never got answered. I made a mental note to dig deeper into the 1998 case.

The library had gone quiet around me, the way old houses do when the light changes. I kept reading.
```

**Rationale:** Josh's reaction is now professional curiosity (podcast material) rather than visceral body memory. Removes dissociative identity tells while maintaining atmospheric tone.

---

### 2. CRITICAL: East Wing Ominous Emphasis (Line ~25)
**Issue:** The phrase "that wing" created a pattern-building breadcrumb for readers to connect Josh to the 1998 murder location.

**Before:**
```
...but the eastern wing—that wing was already in shadow.
```

**After:**
```
...while the opposite side had already fallen into evening shadow.
```

**Rationale:** Removes specific geographic emphasis that could create early pattern recognition. The east wing can still be mentioned in the 1998 case without Josh having an ominous reaction to it on arrival.

---

### 3. MAJOR: Eleanor's "Made This Harder" Softening (Line ~262)
**Issue:** Eleanor showing sympathy for Josh undercut her villain misdirect too early.

**Before:**
```
He seemed... kind. Open. The type who'd be easy to talk to.

That made this harder. The friendly ones always made it harder. They trusted so easily.
```

**After:**
```
He seemed... kind. Open. The type who'd be easy to talk to.

Good. The friendly ones were always easier. They trusted without question, filled silences with their own assumptions, never looked too closely at the person listening.
```

**Rationale:** Eleanor now reads as coldly calculating rather than conflicted. This maintains her villain misdirect through Act 1-2 until the true reveal.

---

### 4. MAJOR: "What If I'm the One Who Can't Be Trusted?" (Line ~4618)
**Issue:** This line read like killer psychology, making Josh=Marcus too obvious.

**Before:**
```
"What if I'm the one who can't be trusted?" he whispers. "What if there's something wrong with me and I don't even know it?"
```

**After:**
```
"I feel like I'm losing my grip," he whispers. "Like there are pieces of me that just... vanish. And I don't know where they go."
```

**Rationale:** Josh's fear is now about losing control/memory (sympathetic) rather than being untrustworthy (killer psychology). The new phrasing is still ominous but reads as confusion rather than guilty conscience.

---

### 5. MAJOR: Eleanor's "Darkness Inside Him" Observation (Line ~3730)
**Issue:** Eleanor explicitly identifying Josh's "inner darkness" read like the author telling us he's the killer.

**Before:**
```
We talk. He tells me about poetry, about his fears. He's afraid of the darkness inside him. That fear makes him vulnerable. Easier to reach. Easier to control.
```

**After:**
```
We talk. He tells me about poetry, about his fears. He's afraid of not finishing his book, of failing the people who believed in him. That fear makes him vulnerable. Easier to reach. Easier to control.
```

**Rationale:** Josh's fears are now mundane creative anxieties rather than "inner darkness." This prevents early twist leakage while maintaining Eleanor's manipulative characterization.
